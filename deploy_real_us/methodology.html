How this map works

This site shows the percentage of days containing a calm-wind window of H consecutive hours below threshold T at 100 m above ground for each of the U.S. licensed and federal spaceports. The model is the same Weibull + Markov pipeline used at calm-winds-spain.netlify.app.

The 5-parameter calm-probability model

Per pixel (at 250 m), we estimate the probability of a calm window using five parameters:

  1. mean — annual mean wind speed (Weibull A scaled by Γ(1+1/k))
  2. k — Weibull shape parameter
  3. diurnal_amp — amplitude of the daily wind cycle
  4. diurnal_phase — UTC hour at which wind is at its minimum
  5. autocorr — lag-1 hour-to-hour persistence (Markov state-transition probability)

Spatial parameters (mean and k) come from GWA at 250 m. Temporal parameters (diurnal_amp, diurnal_phase, autocorr) are computed from the ERA5 hourly time series for the box centered on the spaceport — these vary slowly across a ±0.75° box, so a single time series captures them.

Calm probability at a single hour

Given the five parameters, the probability that wind at hour h is below threshold T is the Weibull CDF:

wind(h) = mean + diurnal_amp · cos(2π·(h − diurnal_phase)/24)
c       = wind(h) / Γ(1 + 1/k)
P(wind < T at hour h) = 1 − exp(−(T/c)k)

Consecutive-hours adjustment

For H > 1 we boost or dampen the single-hour probability by the lag-1 persistence:

persistence_factor = 1 + (autocorr − 0.5) · 0.6     (clamped to [0.5, 1.5])
P(H consecutive hours) = P(1 hour) (H / persistence_factor)

High-autocorrelation regimes (e.g. nocturnal stable boundary layers in deserts) make consecutive calm hours much more likely than the i.i.d. estimate; the formula corrects for this.

Daily aggregation

Finally, we average the per-hour calm probability over the 24 hours of the day, then exponentiate by H/persistence_factor. The result is interpreted as "% of days that contain at least one calm window of H consecutive hours below threshold T".

Layer hierarchy

  • Top map (z3–z7): a single CONUS+AK calm grid computed at 0.5° from ERA5 alone, no GWA refinement. Covers the full country at low resolution.
  • Per-spaceport (z6–z14): GWA-refined 250 m calm grids per site, with optional 10 m DEM terrain refinement at z13+ (lee/windward shading via tanh of TPI deviation).

Caveats

  • One year of ERA5 (2023) drives the temporal parameters. Multi-year averages will follow as the per-site CDS downloads complete.
  • Synoptic time series: the temporal parameters (amp, phase, autocorr) use the box-mean ERA5 time series; sub-bbox spatial variation in those parameters is currently lost. The Spain pipeline computes them per ERA5 cell; we'll port that for Phase D.
  • Downscaling vs simulation: the model is a statistical downscale, not an LES. Expect 0–10 percentage-point local error vs anemometer measurements at any given pixel.
  • Coastal sites: at Cape Canaveral, MARS Wallops, Kodiak, Vandenberg, Boca Chica, the GWA mean wind is biased low over water by ~1 m/s in some pixels (GWA's coastal mask is conservative). Treat over-water calm % cautiously.
